# Invert Sugar

Invert sugar is a sweetener made by breaking down table sugar
(sucrose) into its simpler components, glucose and fructose, through a
process called hydrolysis. It is **sweeter than regular sugar** and is
often used in baked goods, candies, and beverages because it helps
retain moisture and **prevents crystallization**.

 - 400g sugar
 - 237 ml water
 - 1/4 tsp cream of tartar

Put over medium high heat and stir until boiling. Then reduce heat and
let simmer until it reaches 236F (use candy thermometer).

Let cool, then pour into covered jar and refrigerate.
