# Frankenberry Pie

## Filling Ingredients
 - 5 and 1/2 cups (about 780g) fresh berries
 - 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
 - 1/3 cup (41g) all-purpose flour
 - 1 Tablespoon cornstarch
 - 1 Tablespoon lemon juice
 - 1 teaspoon lemon zest

I've used 50/50 blueberries and blackberries+raspberries and that's a
good flavor. I used frozen and that worked, but fresh would probably
give a better texture.

## Streusel Ingredients
 - 1 cup all-purpose flour
 - 1/2 cup brown sugar
 - 1/4 cup granulated sugar
 - 1/4-1/2 tsp cinnamon
 - 1/4 tsp kosher salt
 - 6 tbsp cold butter, diced small

Don't bother doing 2x or even 1.5x, this is plenty for a 9" pie. Also
do not add too much butter for the streusel itself--more butter will
be added for the second baking on top of the pie.

## Crust Ingredients
 - 2 and 1/2 cups (315g) all-purpose flour plus more for shaping and rolling
 - 1 teaspoon salt
 - 6 Tablespoons (85g) unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
 - 2/3 cup (130g) vegetable shortening, chilled
 - 1/2 cup (120ml) ice cold water

This makes two bottom crusts or a bottom and a top. For Frankenberry
Pie, a 1/2 recipe is enough.

## Instructions

Make streusel and crust up to 3 days and not less than 8 hours before
the pie is to be eaten.

### Streusel

 - Preheat oven to 350F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment
   paper.
    
 - In a small bowl combine ingredients.
    
 - Pinch the butter into the mixture with your fingers gently until
   small clumps are formed no larger than the size of a pea. The
   texture should be like coarse wet sand with clumps in it.
    
 - Put it in the freezer for 20 minutes to chill.
    
 - Spread chilled streusel over any baked good and follow recipe
   directions for baking.
    
 - **To bake ahead:** spread the streusel onto a prepared baking sheet
   and bake for 8-10 minutes. *Do not overbake!* Remember this will be
   baked again on top of the pie. If there were larger chunks of
   butter and they melted, that's ok, you'll break it up anyways!
    
### Crust

 - Whisk the flour and salt together in a large bowl.
    
 - Add the butter and shortening. Using a pastry cutter or two forks,
   cut them into the mixture until it resembles coarse meal (pea-sized
   bits with a few larger bits is OK). In this step, you’re only
   breaking up the cold fat into tiny little flour-coated pieces;
   you’re not completely incorporating it. Do not overwork the
   ingredients.
    
 - Measure 1/2 cup (120ml) of water in a cup. Add ice. Stir it
   around. From that, measure 1/2 cup (120ml) of water, since the ice
   has melted a bit. Drizzle the cold water in, 1 tablespoon (15ml) at
   a time, and stir with a rubber spatula or wooden spoon after every
   tablespoon has been added. Stop adding water when the dough begins
   to form large clumps. Do not add any more water than you need.
    
 - Transfer the pie dough to a floured work surface. Using floured
   hands, fold the dough into itself until the flour is fully
   incorporated, *but not fully **mixed***, into the fats. You want
   pockets of butter.

   The dough should come together easily and should not feel overly
   sticky. Avoid overworking the dough. If it feels a bit too dry or
   crumbly, dip your fingers in the ice water and then continue
   bringing dough together with your hands. If it feels too sticky,
   sprinkle on more flour and then continue bringing dough together
   with your hands. Form it into a ball.

 - Use a sharp knife to cut it in half. If it’s helpful, you should
   have about 1 lb, 8 ounces dough total (about 680g). Gently flatten
   each half into 1-inch-thick discs using your hands.
    
 - Wrap each tightly in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours
   and up to 5 days.
    
 - After the dough has chilled for at least 2 hours, you can roll it
   out. Work with one crust at a time, keeping the other in the
   refrigerator until you’re ready to roll it out. Lightly flour the
   work surface, rolling pin, and your hands, and sprinkle a little
   flour on top of the dough. Gently roll the dough into a very thin
   12-inch circle, which is the perfect size to fit a 9-inch pie
   dish. Your pie dough will be about 1/8 inch thick, which is quite
   thin. Visible specks of butter and fat in the dough are perfectly
   normal and expected.
    
 - Transfer the pie crust to the pie dish and trim.

#### to parbake

 - Chill crust in fridge and preheat oven to 375F.

 - When ready, prick crust and fill with pie beads.

 - Place in oven for 12-15m or until golden brown. Remove and allow to
   fully cool before filling.

### Filling and Pie

 - Preheat oven to 400F

 - In a large bowl, mix berries, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and
   zest together until thoroughly combined.

 - Lightly sprinkle flour and cornstarch in while mixing. This avoids
   forming hard clumps of dry powder.

 - Place filling in the refrigerator until needed.
    
 - Spoon the cold filling into the crust and discard the leftover
   juices in the bottom of the bowl, if any

 - Melt a little more butter and mix streusel crumbles into it, then
   sprinkle streusel onto pie.

 - Place the pie onto a large baking sheet and bake for 25
   minutes. Keeping the pie in the oven, turn the temperature down to
   375F and bake for an additional 30-35 minutes. Loosely cover the
   whole pie with aluminum foil to prevent the crumble from browning
   too quickly.

 - Allow the pie to cool for 3 full hours at room temperature before
   serving. This time allows the filling to thicken up.
